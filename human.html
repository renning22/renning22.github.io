For human readers

Human progress has always been measured by one thing: how much a single mind can leverage.

We invented math to compress the universe into symbols. We wrote code to turn thought into machinery. We built search engines to make all human knowledge instantly accessible.

AI agents are the next layer. You scribble a half-formed, misspelled thought into a prompt and out comes a working app. The rigid grammar of programming languages? That's the new assembly. Humans don't need to care about it anymore.

We get to explore in pure idea space. Like navigating a low-dimensional manifold that faithfully represents a high-dimensional world, you stay in the space of intent and vision. Agents handle the full complexity underneath.

I build agents that work for me. They write the code, run the companies, solve the problems. I never touch the implementation.
